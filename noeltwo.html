These are poems that I wrote for both the Child Labor and Social Media Topics. The social media poem is called Lost Connection. The child labor poem is called True Colors

Lost Connection

The sunlight comes in streaming through the window

The light cascading over the bed as if taking its rest for the day

Bare feet thump against the floor, soft skin meeting hardwood

She waits and waits and waits but still, there’s nothing

The treasure that usually hold all the answers she seeks has nothing to offer her today

Nothing has chimed, nothing has buzzed

Desperation floods her as she watches her treasure sink deeper and deeper

Into a dark ocean

Afraid to make the wrong move

Afraid to say the wrong words

She could only wonder how long she has to watch her thoughts drown before finally coming up for air

Finally she sees it, light at the bottom of that sea

She grabs onto it with all she can, keeping sure it stays within her view

The water seems to have answered her call and finally she hits the surface

Taking in all the air she can, she answers back

Pushing at the waves with so much force, she nearly loses herself in the water

Joy envelopes her because now she isn’t going to sink

She can stay afloat with the knowledge that someone was there to keep her up

The connection she thought had sunk

Swimming between what is now clear instead of dark waters

True Colors

Covered in dirt, tired, and overworked,

The little boy wears a smile on his face

But so little joy comes to be so easily replaced

He works the longest hours to provide

But the longer he stays the longer he sets his childhood aside

And decides that it’s not worth it at all

The little boy has his hand resting against a wall

But the rest that is desired doesn’t seem to rest well with the people making the call

To just fix something

To bring home anything

It doesn’t matter how much a child could be suffering

all that matters is the pay

But to pay attention is know that there has to be a way

For someone to just fix something

For someone to bring out anything

Anything that is wrong with the little ways that there are for a child to get hurt come home and still get sent back to work

But he goes on anyway

He goes because there isn’t a day

Where he can’t feel willing, where he can’t feel little

He smiles his biggest smile

The joy he has discovered he knows could only last a little while.
